VISION & IMAGING HISTORY

Where did it all start?

Timeline of Major Events:

1950

The field of computer vision emerged in 1950 with the invention of "perceptron" by Frank Rosenblatt

This machine was designed for image recognition which was reported as the embryo of an electronic computer. It used 20×20 photocells to produce a 400-pixel image. However, it only capable of sorting images into very simple categories like triangle and square.

1970

Marvin Minsky and Seymour Papert improved the algorithm of perceptrons

Studies in the 1970s formed the early foundations for many computer vision algorithms which are used widely till now, including extraction of edges from images, motion estimation, and some basic graph models.

1980

Image processing

This decade saw studies on image processing based on mathematical method, including the concept of blur image, image morphing, graph cutting and shading(same as the photo effects and filters which we are using these days).

1990

eigenface

Things started to heat up in the second half of the 90s due to the improvement on hardware. In this decade, Scientists use geometric approach to achieve face recognition. Toward the end of the 1990s, we saw an increased interaction between the fields of computer graphics and computer vision, like the invention of Medical ultrasound.
