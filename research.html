3D Scene Reconstruction

The objective of this Scene Reconstruction technology is about taking the information of a scene in reality and computing a 3-dimensional model of it. This information provided are usually several pictures or a video of the scene.
First, 3d sensing techniques on a grid basis would be used to get 3d images of the scene. Then certain algorithms would be used to connect the scenes together and form the 3d model.

Recognition

The major problem of all sorts of vision technology is determining whether a specific thing is existing in or is happening in an image. Such problems are categorized into three: Object classification, where the computer has to recognize a certain object that it has learned; Identification, where a unique feature of an object is recognized (eg. fingerprints); Detection, where a certain condition needs to be detected by scanning.
Many brilliant algorithms are used to solve these problems, but still even the best of them struggle to deal with tiny stuff, like a pin. They also struggle with filtered images from most digital cameras.
Specialized tasks based on this technology include:
Content-based image retrieval, used to find images containing a specific content from hundreds of them.
Pose estimate, used to compute the position of an object.
Face recognizing.
Etc.

Cell Phenotype Recognition

The Cell Phenotype Recognition is a type of classifying recognition that UCL focuses in. Its about observing the visible features called phenotypes from a segment of cells and classifying the cells. The process picks out features from the database and labells the cells. After that, all needed to do is to just classify them upon their features. This process sounds simple, but it has to deal with so much data that makes it impossible for manual operation. That's why we need computers to do it for us.

Motion Analysis

This technology is about processing a sequence of images or a video and calculate the velocity of an object or the camera. It supports tasks like tracking (follow movement of an object) and optical flow (determining how an object is moving relative to the scene).

Image-Understanding

Image-understanding system, also called as IUS, contains three levels. The first is called the Low Level. It contains primitives like the sides, the shapes and their position. Going on there is the Intermediate Level containing the volumes, the boundaries, etc. Then there is the High Level which contains the scenes. These require a lot of information from further studies.
Other than that, techniques like inference and control are also used in IUS. Inference simply means to deduce new information from the facts already known, while control is the process that decides which technique should be used and when.

Microstructure Imaging with Diffusion MRI

MRI, full name Magnetic Resonance Imaging, is a type of imaging technique mostly for medical uses, and is also one that UCL focuses on. The system first first recieves a responding signal from the tissue it wants to display (how to obtain the signal is another story so just leave it out), and makes a 3D model of it. It then shows an image of the model and finally goes through a process called fitting to make it display only a certain feature at a time.
